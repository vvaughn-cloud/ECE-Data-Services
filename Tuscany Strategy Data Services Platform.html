U.S. Market Context

Key trends for ECE operators
Under-5 Population — Declined 6.4% from 2019 to 2024 (19.6M → 18.3M). Census projects stabilization near 18.4–18.5M through 2034.
U.S. Fertility Rate — Hit record low of 1.57 births/woman in 2025, down 19.5% from generational average (1973–2010).
Market Consolidation — Large childcare chains grew 14–95% in select states; home-based and independent centers declined 4–17%.
Private Preschool Enrollment — Returned to pre-pandemic levels in 2022–23 for 3–4 year olds. Growth plateau expected.

Case Study: Brand Conversion Success

Background: A chain of 450+ franchised schools in 30 states (60,000 children, PE-owned) acquired a Tier 3 school and needed to transition it to the new brand.

Challenge: Train staff on a more robust curriculum, update indoor and outdoor facilities, and communicate changes to existing families without triggering disenrollment.

Result: Occupancy increased from 47% to 84% over 4 years. Any disenrolled families were replaced with new families, maintaining and increasing occupancy throughout the transition.
